Algorithmic Trading & ML

Predictive Modeling for Large Cap Stocks

Project Philosophy: Interpretability First

The screenshot above shows the live output of my model generating buy signals for the day. This project was not just about predicting price movements, but about understanding the drivers behind them. I adopted a strategy of prioritizing interpretability during experimentation, and only switching to aggressive optimization once the underlying features were proven.

Methodology & Architecture

  • Model Selection Journey: I experimented with a wide range of architectures, including CNNs (treating charts as images), Linear Regression, SVD, and classification models. I initially settled on a Random Forest regressor. Unlike neural networks, Random Forest is a "white box" model. It allowed me to use feature importance metrics to verify which financial indicators (e.g., RSI, MACD, Volume Delta) were actually driving predictions, rather than blindly trusting a black box.
  • Optimization with XGBoost: Once I was confident in my feature set, I migrated the architecture to XGBoost to maximize predictive performance and execution speed.
  • Advanced Tuning Strategy: I implemented a custom two-stage hyperparameter tuning loop. I first used Random Search to explore the global parameter space and identify regions of interest. Once a promising region was found, the system automatically switched to a fine-grained Grid Search to mathematically optimize the model configuration.
  • Data Engineering: I designed a hybrid data storage system. I utilized Parquet files for high-performance I/O during heavy training loads, while maintaining CSV mirrors for easy human readability and debugging during the development phase.

Integration & Deployment

The model is not a static script; it is integrated directly with the Alpaca API. This allows the system to function as a fully autonomous paper-trading bot, pulling live market data, generating inference, and executing buy/sell orders in real-time to validate the strategy against live market conditions.
