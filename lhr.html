Formula Student Data Analysis

Vehicle Dynamics & Telemetry at Longhorn Racing Combustion

Context: FSAE & LHR Combustion

Longhorn Racing (LHR) Combustion is a student-led engineering team that designs, builds, and races a Formula-style race car to compete in the global Formula SAE (Society of Automotive Engineers) competition. In this high-performance environment, design choices—such as adding an aerodynamic package or reducing chassis weight—must be justified by rigorous simulation rather than intuition.

Simulation: Lap Time Sensitivity

The 3D surface plot above visualizes a Lap Time Sensitivity Analysis performed to guide high-level design architecture.

  • The Variables: The plot correlates Coefficient of Drag (Cd) on the X-axis, Added Weight (lbs) on the Y-axis, and the resulting Lap Time (seconds) on the Z-axis (represented by color).
  • The Trade-off: In vehicle dynamics, adding aerodynamic devices (like wings) increases cornering speed but adds both weight and drag. This simulation helps determine the "break-even" point.
  • The Insight: The "Endurance Mode" simulation reveals that lap time is highly sensitive to weight penalties. The gradient shows that even with low drag (low Cd), adding weight pushes the lap time from the efficient purple region (approx 162s) into the slower yellow region (166s+). This data quantified the value of every pound saved on the chassis, influencing our decision to prioritize lightweighting over complex, high-drag aero structures for specific event configurations.

Data Engineering & Pipeline Development

Beyond simulation, my role involved building robust pipelines to process the immense amount of data generated during testing and design validation.

  • Damper Curve Interpolation: I developed algorithms to process force-velocity data from shock dynamometers. Using custom interpolation techniques, I mapped non-linear damper behaviors into our full-vehicle simulations, allowing us to predict transient response over bumps and curbs with high fidelity.
  • Haltech Dyno Data Parsing: I automated the extraction of engine performance data from our Haltech ECU logs. This involved writing scripts to parse raw CSV and binary logs, clean the noisy sensor signals, and inject them into our SQL database for historical trend analysis.
